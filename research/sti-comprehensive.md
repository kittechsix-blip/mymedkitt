## Practice-Changing Updates 2024-2025

### Critical Changes
1. **DoxyPEP approved (June 2024)** - CDC released first guidelines for doxycycline post-exposure prophylaxis for MSM/TGW
2. **Syphilis screening x3 in pregnancy** - ACOG now recommends screening at first visit, third trimester, AND delivery (not risk-based)
3. **Bicillin shortage** - FDA authorized imported alternatives (Lentocilin, Extencilline); prioritize penicillin for pregnant patients
4. **Ceftriaxone resistance rising globally** - 5% resistance in WHO surveillance (up from 0.8% in 2022); US still effective
5. **Metronidazole + alcohol interaction debunked** - No convincing evidence of disulfiram-like reaction
6. **BV partner treatment** - New RCT (2025) shows treating male partners reduces recurrence (35% vs 63%)
7. **Azithromycin no longer co-treatment for gonorrhea** - Ceftriaxone monotherapy is now standard

## Urethritis

### Etiology
- **Gonococcal (GC):** Neisseria gonorrhoeae
- **Non-gonococcal (NGU):** Chlamydia trachomatis (15-40%), Mycoplasma genitalium (15-25%), Trichomonas vaginalis, Ureaplasma urealyticum

### First-Line Treatment

| Syndrome | Treatment | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| **GC urethritis** | Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1 | 1g if >=150kg |
| **Chlamydia** | Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 7 days | First-line (azithromycin now second-line) |
| **Empiric (GC + CT coverage)** | Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d | If CT not ruled out |

### Alternative Regimens
- **Chlamydia:** Azithromycin 1g PO x1 (second-line due to treatment failures)
- **Gonorrhea (if IM not possible):** Cefixime 800mg PO x1 (less reliable for pharyngeal)

### Mycoplasma genitalium
**When resistance testing available:**
- Macrolide-sensitive: Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d, THEN azithromycin 1g day 1, then 500mg daily x 3d (2.5g total)
- Macrolide-resistant: Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d, THEN moxifloxacin 400mg daily x 7d

**When resistance testing NOT available:**
- Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d, THEN moxifloxacin 400mg daily x 7d

**Key Point:** Macrolide resistance is 44-90% in US. Beta-lactams do NOT work (no cell wall).

### Pregnancy Modifications
- **Chlamydia:** Azithromycin 1g PO x1 (doxycycline contraindicated)
- **Gonorrhea:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1 (same as non-pregnant)

### HIV+ Modifications
- Same treatment regimens as HIV-negative patients

### MSM Considerations
- Test all three sites: urethra, rectum, pharynx
- Higher rates of M. genitalium
- Consider doxyPEP discussion (see below)

### Test of Cure
- **Chlamydia:** Retest at 3 months (not test of cure, but screening for reinfection)
- **M. genitalium:** Test of cure 21 days post-treatment if symptoms persist

### Partner Therapy
- Partners within 60 days should be evaluated/treated
- EPT available in 47 states + DC

## Cervicitis

### Etiology
- Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Chlamydia trachomatis
- Also: M. genitalium, T. vaginalis, HSV, BV

### First-Line Treatment
| If | Treatment |
|----|-----------|
| **GC/CT suspected** | Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1 + Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d |
| **CT only** | Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d |

### Pregnancy
- **Chlamydia:** Azithromycin 1g PO x1
- **Gonorrhea:** Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1

### Recurrent Cervicitis
- Test for M. genitalium
- Consider BV co-infection

## Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

### Diagnostic Criteria (Minimum)
Sexually active woman with pelvic/lower abdominal pain AND one of:
- Cervical motion tenderness
- Uterine tenderness
- Adnexal tenderness

### Additional Criteria (Increase Specificity)
- Temp >101F (>38.3C)
- Mucopurulent cervical discharge
- WBCs on wet mount
- Elevated ESR/CRP
- Positive GC/CT test

### Hospitalization Criteria
- Pregnancy
- Tubo-ovarian abscess
- Severe illness (high fever, N/V, temp >38.5C)
- Unable to tolerate/comply with outpatient regimen
- No clinical improvement after 72h outpatient therapy
- Possible surgical emergency cannot be excluded

### Treatment Regimens

#### Outpatient (Mild-Moderate)
**Recommended:**
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1 (1g if >=150kg)
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 14 days
- PLUS Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 14 days

**Alternative:**
- Cefoxitin 2g IM x1 WITH Probenecid 1g PO x1
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 14 days
- PLUS Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 14 days

**2021 Update:** Metronidazole now ROUTINELY added (previously optional) for anaerobic coverage and BV treatment.

#### Inpatient (Parenteral)
**Recommended:**
- Ceftriaxone 1g IV q24h
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV q12h
- PLUS Metronidazole 500mg IV/PO q12h

**Alternative:**
- Cefotetan 2g IV q12h + Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV q12h, OR
- Cefoxitin 2g IV q6h + Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV q12h

**Transition to oral:** After 24-48h clinical improvement, complete 14-day total course with oral doxycycline + metronidazole.

### Pregnancy
- **Hospitalize all pregnant patients with PID**
- Doxycycline contraindicated - use azithromycin alternative

### IUD Management
- **Do NOT remove IUD**
- Treat according to guidelines
- Reassess in 48-72h

### Follow-Up
- Reevaluate in 72h if no improvement
- Test all partners within 60 days

## Genital Ulcers

### Differential Diagnosis
| Etiology | Characteristics |
|----------|-----------------|
| **HSV** | Painful vesicles/ulcers, may recur, bilateral |
| **Syphilis (chancre)** | Painless, indurated, clean base, single |
| **Chancroid** | Painful, purulent, ragged edges, inguinal adenopathy |
| **LGV** | Small painless papule -> regional lymphadenopathy |

### Diagnostic Workup
- HSV PCR/NAAT from lesion
- Darkfield microscopy or NAAT for syphilis
- Syphilis serology (RPR/VDRL + treponemal test)
- HSV type-specific serology
- HIV testing (all patients with genital ulcers)

### Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV)

#### First Episode
| Drug | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| Acyclovir | 400mg PO TID | 7-10 days |
| Valacyclovir | 1g PO BID | 7-10 days |
| Famciclovir | 250mg PO TID | 7-10 days |

Extend if healing incomplete at 10 days.

#### Recurrent Episodes (Episodic Therapy)
| Drug | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| Acyclovir | 400mg PO TID | 5 days |
| Acyclovir | 800mg PO BID | 5 days |
| Valacyclovir | 500mg PO BID | 3 days |
| Valacyclovir | 1g PO daily | 5 days |
| Famciclovir | 125mg PO BID | 5 days |

Start within 24h of symptom onset or during prodrome.

#### Suppressive Therapy (>=6 recurrences/year)
| Drug | Dose |
|------|------|
| Acyclovir | 400mg PO BID |
| Valacyclovir | 500mg PO daily (1g if >=10 episodes/yr) |
| Famciclovir | 250mg PO BID |

Reduces recurrences by 70-80% and transmission by ~50%.

#### Pregnancy
- Acyclovir and valacyclovir safe in pregnancy
- Suppressive therapy from 36 weeks recommended if history of genital herpes

#### HIV+
- May need higher doses or longer duration
- Suppressive therapy reduces HIV transmission risk

### Chancroid
**Treatment:**
- Azithromycin 1g PO x1, OR
- Ceftriaxone 250mg IM x1, OR
- Ciprofloxacin 500mg PO BID x 3d, OR
- Erythromycin base 500mg PO TID x 7d

**HIV+:** May need longer courses; monitor closely.

### Lymphogranuloma Venereum (LGV)
**Treatment:**
- Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 21 days (longer than regular chlamydia)

**Alternative:**
- Erythromycin base 500mg PO QID x 21 days

**Partners:** Treat with doxycycline 100mg BID x 7 days

## Proctitis

### Etiology (MSM)
- Neisseria gonorrhoeae
- Chlamydia trachomatis (including LGV serovars)
- HSV
- Treponema pallidum

### Diagnostic Workup
- Rectal NAAT for GC/CT
- LGV PCR if available
- HSV NAAT
- Syphilis serology
- Consider anoscopy

### Treatment

#### Empiric (Before Results)
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 7 days (extend to 21 days if LGV suspected)

#### If LGV Confirmed/Suspected
- Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x **21 days**

#### HSV Proctitis
- Acyclovir 400mg PO TID x 7-10 days, OR
- Valacyclovir 1g PO BID x 7-10 days

### MSM-Specific Considerations
- Higher prevalence of LGV proctitis
- HSV more common with HIV co-infection
- Consider mpox in differential (see below)
- Screen for all STIs including syphilis

## Pharyngeal Infections

### Key Points
- More difficult to eradicate than urogenital/rectal
- **NO reliable alternative to ceftriaxone**
- Test of cure REQUIRED

### Treatment
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1 (ONLY recommended regimen)
- If chlamydia not excluded: add Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7d

### Test of Cure
- **Required for ALL pharyngeal gonorrhea**
- Timing: 7-14 days post-treatment
- Method: Culture preferred (with NAAT); if NAAT positive, culture before retreatment

### Treatment Failure
- Ceftriaxone 1g IM + Azithromycin 2g PO
- Perform antimicrobial susceptibility testing
- Report to health department

## Vaginitis

### Trichomonas vaginalis

#### First-Line Treatment
| Population | Treatment |
|------------|-----------|
| Women | Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 7 days |
| Men | Metronidazole 2g PO x1 |
| Women with HIV | Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 7 days |

**2021 Update:** 7-day regimen now preferred for women (single dose had higher failure rate).

#### Alternative
- Tinidazole 2g PO x1
- Secnidazole 2g PO x1

#### Treatment Failure
- If no reexposure: Metronidazole or tinidazole 2g PO daily x 7 days
- Resistance testing available through CDC

#### Pregnancy
- Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 7 days (safe in all trimesters)
- Tinidazole: avoid in pregnancy

#### Partner Treatment
- Treat ALL partners - required for cure
- EPT recommended

### Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)

**Note:** Not technically an STI but frequently co-occurs.

#### First-Line Treatment
- Metronidazole 500mg PO BID x 7 days, OR
- Metronidazole gel 0.75% intravaginally daily x 5 days, OR
- Clindamycin cream 2% intravaginally at bedtime x 7 days

#### Alternative
- Clindamycin 300mg PO BID x 7 days
- Secnidazole 2g PO x1
- Tinidazole 2g PO daily x 2 days

#### Alcohol + Metronidazole
**2021 Update:** No convincing evidence of disulfiram-like interaction. Alcohol restriction during metronidazole is unnecessary.

#### Pregnancy
- Treat all symptomatic pregnant women
- Metronidazole safe in pregnancy
- Avoid tinidazole

#### Partner Treatment
- Traditionally NOT recommended
- **2025 RCT:** Partner treatment reduced recurrence (35% vs 63% at 12 weeks)

#### Recurrent BV
- Metronidazole gel 0.75% twice weekly for 4-6 months, OR
- Metronidazole/tinidazole x7d -> boric acid 600mg vaginal suppository daily x21d -> metronidazole gel twice weekly x4-6mo

## Epididymitis/Orchitis

### Etiology by Age/Risk
| Population | Likely Pathogens |
|------------|------------------|
| Sexually active <35 | GC, CT |
| MSM (insertive anal sex) | GC, CT, AND enteric bacteria |
| Men >35 or recent instrumentation | Enteric bacteria (E. coli, Pseudomonas) |

### Treatment

#### STI-Related (GC/CT Likely)
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 10 days

#### MSM with Insertive Anal Sex (GC/CT + Enteric)
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM x1
- PLUS Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 10 days
- Consider adding levofloxacin if enteric organisms suspected

#### Enteric Organisms Only
- Levofloxacin 500mg PO daily x 10 days

### Hospitalization Indications
- Severe pain suggesting torsion/abscess
- Unable to comply with outpatient treatment
- Fever with systemic symptoms

### Partner Management
- Evaluate/treat partners within 60 days if STI etiology

## Syphilis

### Staging and Treatment

| Stage | Duration | Treatment |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Primary** | Chancre present | Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM x1 |
| **Secondary** | Rash, mucous patches | Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM x1 |
| **Early Latent** | <1 year, asymptomatic | Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM x1 |
| **Late Latent** | >1 year or unknown duration | Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM weekly x 3 weeks |
| **Tertiary** | Gummas, cardiovascular | Benzathine PCN G 2.4 MU IM weekly x 3 weeks |
| **Neurosyphilis** | Any CNS involvement | Aqueous PCN G 18-24 MU/day IV x 10-14 days |

### Neurosyphilis/Ocular/Otosyphilis
**Recommended:**
- Aqueous crystalline penicillin G 18-24 MU/day IV (3-4 MU q4h or continuous infusion) x 10-14 days

**Alternative:**
- Procaine PCN 2.4 MU IM daily + Probenecid 500mg PO QID x 10-14 days

Consider adding benzathine PCN weekly x 1-3 doses after completing neurosyphilis treatment.

### Pregnancy
- **Penicillin is the ONLY effective treatment**
- Screen at first visit, third trimester, AND delivery
- Desensitize if penicillin allergic
- Treat >=30 days before delivery for best outcomes

### Penicillin Allergy (Non-Pregnant)
- **Early syphilis:** Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 14 days
- **Late latent:** Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 28 days
- **Neurosyphilis:** Desensitize and treat with penicillin (no alternatives)

### Bicillin Shortage (2024-2026)
- FDA authorized imported alternatives: Lentocilin, Extencilline
- **Prioritize penicillin for pregnant patients**
- Non-pregnant patients: doxycycline acceptable alternative
- Expected recovery: Q3 2026

### Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction
- Acute febrile reaction within 24h of treatment
- More common in early syphilis
- Manage supportively (antipyretics, hydration)
- Does NOT indicate allergy - do not discontinue treatment

### Follow-Up Serology
- Monitor RPR/VDRL at 6 and 12 months (24 months for late latent)
- Fourfold decline expected by 6-12 months
- Failure to decline: evaluate for neurosyphilis, consider retreatment

## Doxycycline PEP (DoxyPEP)

### CDC Guidelines (June 2024)

**Who Should Be Offered DoxyPEP:**
- Gay/bisexual men (MSM) with bacterial STI in past 12 months
- Transgender women with bacterial STI in past 12 months
- Can discuss with MSM/TGW without recent STI who engage in high-risk activities

**Efficacy:**
- Syphilis: 73-87% reduction
- Chlamydia: 70-88% reduction
- Gonorrhea: ~50% reduction

**Dosing:**
- Doxycycline 200mg PO as soon as possible, within 72 hours after oral, vaginal, or anal sex
- Maximum: 200mg in 24 hours

**Monitoring:**
- Baseline STI screening
- STI testing every 3-6 months at all sites of exposure
- Reassess need for doxyPEP every 3-6 months

**NOT Recommended For:**
- Cisgender women
- Cisgender heterosexual men
- Transgender men
(Insufficient data in these populations)

**Concerns:**
- Potential for antimicrobial resistance
- Does not replace condom use
- Does not prevent HIV (continue PrEP if indicated)

## Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT)

### Current Legal Status (2024)
- **Permissible:** 47 states + DC
- **Not allowed:** Kansas, South Dakota
- **Recent change:** Oklahoma allowed EPT effective November 2024

### Indications
- Chlamydia
- Gonorrhea
- Trichomonas (in some states)

### EPT Regimens
| Infection | EPT Regimen |
|-----------|-------------|
| Chlamydia | Doxycycline 100mg BID x 7 days |
| Gonorrhea | Cefixime 800mg PO x1 (if IM not possible) |
| Trichomonas | Metronidazole 2g PO x1 |

### Key Points
- Patient provides medication/prescription to partner
- Partner should still be evaluated if possible
- Document EPT in medical record
- Inform patient about drug allergies, side effects

## Mpox Considerations

### When to Consider Mpox in STI Workup
- MSM or transgender women with new skin lesions
- Anyone with genital/perianal ulcers
- High local mpox incidence

### Clinical Features
- Skin lesions (may mimic STIs)
- Can present as proctitis
- Systemic symptoms (fever, lymphadenopathy)

### Co-Infection
- 41% of mpox patients reported STI in prior year
- 38% had HIV at time of diagnosis
- **Mpox does NOT rule out STI - test for both**

### Testing
- PCR from lesion swabs (2-3 swabs)
- Not indicated for asymptomatic screening

### Management
- Mostly supportive care
- Tecovirimat available but efficacy not proven
- Isolation until lesions crusted and healed

### Vaccination (JYNNEOS)
Indicated for:
- MSM at risk (new STI diagnosis, multiple partners, commercial sex venues)
- Known/suspected mpox exposure
- Pregnancy is NOT a contraindication

## Antimicrobial Resistance Updates

### Gonorrhea
**Global (WHO 2024):**
- Ceftriaxone resistance: 5% (up from 0.8% in 2022)
- Cefixime resistance: 11%
- Azithromycin resistance: 4%
- Ciprofloxacin resistance: 95%
- Vietnam hotspot: 27-32% ceftriaxone resistance

**United States:**
- No confirmed treatment failures to ceftriaxone
- Continue monitoring through GISP/CARGOS

**Pipeline:**
- Gepotidacin and zoliflodacin in development
- May be less reliable for pharyngeal infections

### Mycoplasma genitalium
- Macrolide resistance: 44-90% in US
- Quinolone resistance: lower but emerging
- Use resistance-guided therapy when possible

### Trichomonas
- Metronidazole resistance: 4-10%
- Tinidazole resistance: ~1%
- CDC offers resistance testing
